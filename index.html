HOME PORTFOLIO NOTES
Hi! I'm Igor and I am a problem solving enthusiast. My favorite way of solving problems is programming - from electronics to abstract math. I started my programming journey with Arduino when I was around 15 and I remember that it was quite hard to get myself to keep my interest in anything - blinking LED's is exciting for the first fifteen minutes. It was until I got the idea to make my sister a lamp that would turn on with a clap. I went crazy about it - I learned how to solder and started to really learn programming. I did not realise how formative seeing someone excited at something I made really was. After that I wanted to one-up myself and for my personal project at school I made an automatic shifter for a bike. This was next level for me - from understanding how derailleur works through building bracket for a motor to writing code - it was amazing. After that I wanted to try some "real" programming so I played around with C and C++ with some simple project, but like in the case of Arduino it was boring to write code that serves little to no purpose. At the time I was attending a computer science class and we were writing pseudocode - it was very obnoxious as in the existing tool there was no error detection and you had to inspect the code for the littlest typo. That's why I decided to write an interpreter. I fired up Vim, copied CMakeList from StackOverflow and got to work. In the span of three months I went from barely understanding what a pointer is to simulating a call stack. Suddenly compilers and interpreters stopped being a black box to me but also made me realise how ignorant I am. Now I am more hungry for knowledge than ever and keep on exploring the world of abstraction.

Hi! My name is Igor and I love solving problems and building stuff. I am going for breadth rather than depth, hence my projects are all over the place - from automatic bike shifter to an interpreter.
